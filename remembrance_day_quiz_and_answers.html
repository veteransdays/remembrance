Each year on November 2, Catholics around the world observe All Souls’ Day, a day dedicated to prayer, remembrance, and almsgiving for the souls in Purgatory. According to Catholic teaching, Purgatory is a place—or state—of purification for the souls of the faithful departed who are not yet ready to behold God in heaven.
