Most, but not all, employees who work on Remembrance Day and who have worked on at least 15 of the 30 calendar days immediately before Remembrance Day are entitled to receive a holiday with pay. Employees who do not work on Remembrance Day do not get paid for the day. Employees do not earn premium pay (e.g., time and a half) for the time worked on the holiday under the Remembrance Day Act (PDF). However, employers may provide a greater benefit of premium pay under a contract of employment (e.g., a collective agreement).
