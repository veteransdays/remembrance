The Sikh Remembrance Day Ceremony takes place at Private Buckam Singh's military grave, at Mount Hope Cemetery in Kitchener, Ontario. Now in its seventh year, the ceremony has become one of the largest annual gatherings of active serving Sikh soldiers and Veterans in North America. The Sikh Remembrance Day Ceremony has become a cherished tradition, honoring the service and sacrifices of Sikh soldiers like Buckam Singh who fought alongside their Canadian counterparts in World War I. The ceremony was created to honour Private Buckam Singh who was one of nine Sikh soldiers that served in the Canadian Army during the First World War. “As a historian, I spent a lot of time researching Private Singh’s life and his experiences,” said Sandeep Singh Brar who is the Sikh Remembrance Day Chief Organizer. A solemn ceremony was held for a Sikh war hero on Sunday November 5, 2023, at a Kitchener cemetery.
