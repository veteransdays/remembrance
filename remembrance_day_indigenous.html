According to the Canadian Labour Standards Code, when Remembrance Day occurs on a Saturday, the employer must grant his or her employee a holiday with pay on the next working day immediately preceding or following the holiday. Employees who work on Remembrance Day are paid for at least half of a normal work day at 1 ½ times their regular wages. For example, employees who work two hours on Remembrance Day, but normally work an eight-hour day, are paid four hours at 1 ½ times their regular wage.
