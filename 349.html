Every year, Oshawa Power hangs Remembrance Day banners in downtown Oshawa to honour members of the community who served in the Canadian military. This year, Oshawa Power added five new banners in honour of Oshawa resident Terri Normoyle’s father, uncles and brother. In advance of this year’s Remembrance Day Parade and Ceremony at Oshawa’s Memorial Park, Oshawa Power has installed 101 banners honouring and memorializing Oshawa’s veterans. You can see them around the downtown core. Oshawa is hosting an in-person Remembrance Day event on Friday, Nov. 11. Join Royal Canadian Legion Branch 43 and Royal Canadian Legion Branch 637 for a parade at 10:30 a.m. followed by a service at the cenotaph in Memorial Park at 11 a.m. Locally, hundreds of residents gather at the cenotaph in Memorial Park in downtown Oshawa for a Remembrance Day parade and ceremony. A person lays a poppy on the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, after the National Remembrance Day Ceremony at the National War Memorial in Ottawa, on Saturday, Nov. 11, 2023. An army of volunteers has been polishing up old Second World War Jeeps, trucks and other military vehicles that will be part of a special convoy of remembrance in Oshawa on Friday. The annual Oshawa Remembrance Day parade and service takes place this Monday, Nov. 11, from about 10:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. in downtown Oshawa. Run by the two Oshawa branches of the Royal Canadian Legion, branches 43 and 637, it is also supported by many community partners.
