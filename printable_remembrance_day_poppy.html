Remembrance Day (also known as Poppy Day owing to the tradition of wearing a remembrance poppy) is a memorial day observed in Commonwealth member states since the end of the First World War to honour armed forces members who have died in the line of duty. [1] Ontario remains one of the few provinces where Remembrance Day is not a statutory holiday. Manitoba and Quebec also do not give workers holiday pay or the day off.
