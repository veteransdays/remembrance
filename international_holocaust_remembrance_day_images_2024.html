It is also a statutory holiday in three territories (Yukon, Northwest Territories and Nunavut) and in six provinces (British Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchewan, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island and Newfoundland and Labrador). Employees who work on Remembrance Day are paid for at least half of a normal work day at 1 ½ times their regular wages.
